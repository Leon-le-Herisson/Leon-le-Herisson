## 🕵️‍♂️ whoami 

I did not come from a neat, linear, polished path built to impress recruiters.

I studied, changed directions, explored different worlds, and kept moving.
From `med school` to `psychology & Ericksonian hypnotherapy`, from `sport` to `travel`, from `human complexity` to `software logic`, the common thread has stayed the same:
*understand deeply, think clearly, and build something solid*.

Fun fact: *apparently, I'm a boomer*.

- human understanding
- software development

## 🧘🏼‍♂️ about_me

I like complex systems.

Sometimes they are written in C and refuse to behave. Sometimes they are human and keep repeating the same patterns while hoping for a different outcome. Either way, the work is similar: observe, understand, adjust, test, and rebuild cleanly.

What probably defines me best is this mix:

- analytical thinking  
- directness  
- curiosity  
- discipline  
- empathy  
- a refusal of decorative nonsense when substance is missing  

I prefer projects with depth. The kind that force you to think, fail, refine, and come back stronger.

## 🧬 beyond_code

### Psychology / clinical thinking
My background in psychology and Ericksonian hypnotherapy shaped the way I approach problems: listen first, identify the underlying structure, understand the function, then intervene with precision, case by case.

### Sport
Sport has been a major part of my life, and I still practice basketball and climbing every week.

It taught me consistency, effort, body awareness, frustration tolerance, and the difference between wanting something and actually doing the work.

### Travel
Travel helped me widen my perspective and question what people casually call “normal”. It also gave me a clearer view of my own life and of what truly matters to me.
